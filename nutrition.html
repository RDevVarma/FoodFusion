Nutritional Facts

Understanding what makes a good diet is essential for maintaining a healthy lifestyle. Here are some key components of a balanced diet:

Calories

Calories are the units of energy you get from food and drink. Your body needs calories to function and to fuel activities. Consuming the right amount of calories is crucial to maintaining a healthy weight.

Macronutrients

Macronutrients are the nutrients you need in larger quantities that provide you with energy. They include:

  • Proteins: Essential for growth, repair, and maintenance of body tissues. Good sources include meat, fish, beans, and nuts.
  • Carbohydrates: The body's main source of energy. Found in bread, rice, pasta, and fruits.
  • Fats: Necessary for energy, cell function, and absorption of vitamins. Healthy sources include avocados, nuts, and olive oil.

Vitamins

Vitamins are essential for various bodily functions, including metabolism, immunity, and digestion. Key vitamins include:

  • Vitamin A: Important for vision and immune function. Found in carrots, sweet potatoes, and spinach.
  • Vitamin C: Necessary for the growth and repair of tissues. Found in citrus fruits, strawberries, and bell peppers.
  • Vitamin D: Crucial for bone health. Can be obtained from sunlight exposure, fortified foods, and fatty fish.

Minerals

Minerals are important for bone health, fluid balance, and muscle function. Key minerals include:

  • Calcium: Essential for strong bones and teeth. Found in dairy products, leafy greens, and fortified foods.
  • Iron: Necessary for blood production. Good sources include red meat, beans, and fortified cereals.
  • Potassium: Important for muscle function and fluid balance. Found in bananas, potatoes, and yogurt.

Water

Staying hydrated is vital for overall health. Water is essential for digestion, absorption of nutrients, and regulation of body temperature.
